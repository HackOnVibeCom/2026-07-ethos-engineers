# 3-Minute Demo Script (exact click-through)

**Prep before recording:** create one app in advance ("HabitDeck — a habit tracker that turns your streaks into a card game") with all 5 channels + plan generated, and 3-4 tracking entries logged (e.g. Product Hunt 38 installs, Reddit 24, X 11, LinkedIn 3). Have a SECOND blank tab on the intake form. Practice once; this script runs ~2:50.

---

**[0:00–0:20] The problem** *(on the landing page)*

> "Indie devs are great at building apps and terrible at promoting them. Most newly launched apps die with zero visibility — not because they're bad, but because the dev never writes the App Store copy, the launch thread, the Product Hunt pitch. LaunchCopilot fixes that."

**[0:20–0:40] Intake** *(fill the form live — fast)*

> "I give it what any dev already knows: name, category, one-line pitch, who it's for. Twenty seconds of input."

Click **Generate my launch kit** → land on dashboard → click **⚡ Generate full kit**. While it generates, switch to the pre-made HabitDeck dashboard.

**[0:40–1:40] THE MONEY SHOT — channel-aware copy side-by-side** *(pre-made app, scroll slowly)*

> "Here's the same app across five channels — and this is the point: it's not one blurb reused everywhere."

- Show **App Store**: "Title under 30 characters, a real 100-character keyword field, description hooked for the 3-line fold. ASO rules, not vibes."
- Show **Product Hunt**: "A sub-60-character tagline and a maker's first comment with the origin story and a feedback ask — the exact structure PH users expect."
- Show **Reddit**: "And look at the difference — it picked r/androidapps, the title is 'I built this because…', it discloses I'm the dev, invites criticism, and warns me to check the sub's self-promo rules. Marketing voice here gets you banned; this won't."

> "Every output knows its platform's conventions. That's the AI depth — one input, five native voices."

Click one **Copy** button: "Everything is one click from being live."

**[1:40–2:10] The 7-day plan** *(scroll to plan)*

> "Copy without a sequence still fails, so it generates a 7-day plan tuned to the category: Product Hunt and the X thread on day one for momentum, Reddit mid-week when I can actually answer comments, ASO iteration on day five after real signal. Each task is ~20 minutes."

**[2:10–2:35] Tracking + the optimizer — THE closing argument** *(scroll to chart)*

> "Then I log installs per channel — and here's the part no copywriting tool does: I hit 'Rewrite my weakest channel using this data'. The AI reads my actual results, forms a hypothesis — here it decided my LinkedIn hook was too product-centric for a career network — and rewrites the copy with a changelog. My launch data literally becomes the next prompt. Generate → post → measure → improve."

*(Pre-run the optimizer before recording so the blue "Optimized from your data" box is already on one card — click the button live only if generation is reliably under 15s.)*

**[2:35–2:55] Business + close — show the paywall WORKING**

*(Switch to the blank tab, try to create a second app → the upgrade wall appears → click through to /pricing → hit Upgrade → Pro activates.)*

> "And the business model isn't a slide — it's built. First launch kit free; the second app hits this paywall: ₹499 a month for unlimited apps, regeneration, and optimizations. A kit costs me under ₹6 to serve, so margins are 80%+ from user one. My first customers are in this Discord — every one of you has an app that needs promoting. Link's below — roast my copy."

*(Demo tip: use the "Reset to free (demo)" button on /pricing between rehearsals so the paywall triggers again.)*

## Recording tips
- 1080p screen recording, hide bookmarks bar, dark browser theme (matches UI).
- Do the fast intake on the blank app FIRST take; if generation is slow, you're already covered by the pre-made app switch at 0:40.
- Post the video + live link in the HackOnVibe Discord immediately — ask people to submit THEIR app through it and reply with what the Reddit output gave them (instant Community Validation evidence: screenshots of others' kits).
